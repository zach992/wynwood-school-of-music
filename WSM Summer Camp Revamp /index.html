What your child will walk away with

Every camper — beginner or intermediate — leaves with measurable progress, new friendships, and their first live performance in the books.

For First-Time Campers

Beginners will leave able to…

  • Play a full song on stage with a live band.Not a sit-down recital — an amplified performance with peers.
  • Hold their own in a rehearsal.Count time, follow a leader, know when to come in and when to lay back.
  • Read basic chord charts and rhythms.The foundation of every song they'll ever want to learn next.
  • Feel confident picking up an instrument again.The #1 reason we hear from returning parents.
For Returning & Intermediate Players

Intermediate players will…

  • Play a featured solo in a showcase set.Stretch past the song chart and improvise in front of an audience.
  • Learn to lead a band.Count in a song, cue transitions, and read the room like a pro.
  • Deepen music theory fluency.Scales, modes, and ear-training applied to repertoire they choose.
  • Write & arrange original material.Optional track for campers ready to move beyond covers.

Answers to all your questions

    Does my child need prior experience playing an instrument?
    Absolutely not. The camp is for every skill level. Beginners and advanced students are placed into small groups with musicians at a similar level — no one gets lost and no one gets held back.
    Which instruments can my child enroll in?
    Voice, guitar, keyboard, bass, and drums. If your child plays something else and wants to join, email us — we can often accommodate.
    Does my child need to bring their own instrument?
    We encourage it (practicing on the same instrument you rehearse on matters), but we have loaner instruments on a first-come basis for families who don't have one yet.
    How does registration and payment work?
    You pick your sessions above and check out online with a 50% deposit. The remaining balance is charged to the card on file on the first day of each session. No phone tag, no contact forms.
    What's your cancellation policy?
    Cancel 14+ days before your session starts and you get a full refund of the deposit. 7–13 days: 50% refund. 0–6 days: the deposit is non-refundable. Family illness is handled case-by-case — just call us.
    Will there be lunch and breaks?
    Yes. Lunch + Community Hour runs 12:15–1:15 daily. Kids bring their own lunch or order delivery as a group. This is also when friendships form — which, no exaggeration, is half the reason kids come back.
    Do you offer aftercare?
    Yes — 3:30–5:00 PM, $25/day, no commitment. Add it day-of or for the whole week. Private lessons after camp are also available (separate program/cost).
    Where can we park?
    A parking lot is available along the western wall of the school. Additional parking along 29th Street and 13th Ave.
