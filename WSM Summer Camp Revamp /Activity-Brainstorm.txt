WSM SUMMER CAMP — "BUILD A BAND" ACTIVITY BRAINSTORM
=====================================================

Core framing: camp isn't "sit in a chair and practice 8 hours a day."
It's "build a band" — every activity has a visible link back to the
band's identity, performance, or music. Kids enjoy them independently
but they all reinforce the theme.


A · BAND IDENTITY (the "who are we" stuff)
------------------------------------------
Done early in the week so the band has something to rally around.

 1. Name Your Band — collaborative naming exercise Monday afternoon
    (counselor-moderated; silly names are features, not bugs)
 2. Design Your Band Logo — on paper first, then stylized
 3. Write Your Band's Origin Story — fake backstory (e.g., "we met in
    a Wynwood alleyway during a mural stakeout"); they read it at the
    Friday show
 4. Band Motto / Slogan — goes on the banner
 5. Band Handshake — invent one together, perform it onstage


B · WEARABLES & MERCH (kids love this)
--------------------------------------
Merch feels professional and gives parents something to photograph.

 6. Tie-Dye Band T-Shirts — whole band wears them Friday
 7. Iron-On Patches — with their logo, for denim jackets/backpacks
 8. Pin-Back Buttons — button-maker, everyone goes home with 3–5
 9. Custom Stickers — design them, slap them, hand out at the show
10. Wristbands for the show — they design them, printed for Friday
    audience
11. Tote Bags — blank canvas tote, they paint/draw their band's art


C · STAGE & SHOW DESIGN (the visible payoff)
--------------------------------------------
This stuff actually ends up at the Friday show — high impact,
parents see it.

12. Band Banner — big fabric/paper backdrop for the Friday stage
13. Hand-Painted Tour Poster — displayed at showcase entrance; kids
    can take home a copy
14. Concert Program / Playbill — printed program handed to parents
    Friday
15. Setlist Art — each band's setlist, decoratively drawn, printed
    and taped to the floor stage-right (like real shows)
16. Backstage Pass Lanyards — for the band and for VIP parents
17. Marquee Sign — old-school paper marquee with band name over the
    stage door


D · "ALBUM" EXPERIENCE (pretend you're a real band)
---------------------------------------------------
Roleplay as a working band. Kids love the suspension of disbelief.

18. Album Cover Art — design a fake album for their band (covers
    often feature their tie-dye shirts or banner art)
19. Album Liner Notes — credits, thank-yous, hidden inside jokes
20. Music Video Storyboard — draw out shots for a music video of
    their song (optional stretch: counselors film clips)
21. Fake Magazine Cover — their band on Rolling Stone /
    Alternative Press / Miami New Times
22. Band "Rider" — the silly backstage demand list (green M&Ms etc.)
    — posted in the green room all week


E · COMPETITIONS / HIGH ENERGY (breaks the day up)
--------------------------------------------------
Short, energetic, loud. Keeps the week fun between rehearsals.

23. Name That Tune — counselors DJ, rounds throughout the week
24. Air Band Lip-Sync Battle — bands lip-sync a song, others vote
25. Songwriting-Off — 30 minutes to write a chorus, then perform it
26. Instrument Relay — 60 seconds per instrument, rotate through a
    song
27. Stump the Counselor — kids try to name songs counselors don't
    know
28. Music Trivia Lunch — trivia during lunch every day, running
    leaderboard


F · BONDING / SILLY (the "half the reason kids come back" stuff)
----------------------------------------------------------------
The stuff that makes it a camp, not a class.

29. Silent Disco — headphones, DJ sets during lunch one day
30. Karaoke Hour — low-stakes Friday-morning warm-up
31. Pre-Show Huddle — every band develops their own chant/cheer
32. Photo Booth — at the Friday showcase, with band prop boxes
33. Mock Band Interview — counselor plays reporter, silly questions
34. Iconic Video Dress-Up Day — come dressed like a character from a
    famous music video


G · SPECIAL EXPERIENCES (sell-the-week moments)
-----------------------------------------------
The "wait that happens at your camp?" moments that parents tell
other parents about.

35. Surprise Guest Musician Drop-In — a local Miami pro stops by
    once a session
36. Wynwood Walls Walk — quick walk around the murals; framed as
    "album cover inspiration"
37. Instrument Petting Zoo — weird instruments day (theremin,
    melodica, cajón, kazoo)
38. Looper Pedal Demo — mind-blowing for most kids; they try it
39. Basic DJ / Mixing Lesson — 30-min intro
40. Record Studio Tour — if logistically possible (or show the
    school's own recording setup)


H · END-OF-WEEK KEEPSAKES (parent-facing)
-----------------------------------------
What they take home matters for word-of-mouth.

41. Band Yearbook — printed keepsake: group photo, band bio, quotes,
    setlist, signed by all campers + instructors
42. "Gold Record" Certificate — signed by instructors, framed-style,
    with the band name
43. Group Composite Band Photo — with instruments, the wall where
    the banner hangs
44. MP3 Recording of the Friday Show — emailed to parents Monday
    morning (huge parent wow)
45. Showcase Highlight Reel — short edited video, social-ready


I · VISUAL ARTS TIED TO MUSIC
-----------------------------

46. Paint to Music — free painting session with instrumental music
    playing
47. "Draw Your Song" — what does your song look like? abstract art
48. Music Zine — small magazine about their band, photocopied,
    handed out
49. Lyric Art — hand-lettered lyric poster of their featured song


J · CREATIVE WRITING
--------------------

50. Write Your Own Band's Song (intermediate track) — even just a
    verse & chorus
51. Fake Grammy Acceptance Speech — they deliver it at Friday show
    if they choose
52. Song "Cover Letter" — why did your band pick this song? written,
    shared


=====================================================
MY TOP-10 PICKS TO FEATURE ON THE PAGE
=====================================================

If forced to pick the ones that best sell "this is a
transformation, not a class," these are the ones where every
activity has a visible artifact (shirt, banner, poster, yearbook) —
which is gold for parents telling other parents, and for Instagram.

 1. Name Your Band (Monday)
 2. Tie-Dye Band T-Shirts (worn Friday)
 3. Paint Your Band Banner (onstage Friday)
 4. Design Your Band Logo + Pin-Back Buttons
 5. Hand-Painted Tour Poster / Concert Program
 6. Mock Magazine Cover / Album Cover Art
 7. Surprise Guest Musician Drop-In
 8. Instrument Petting Zoo
 9. Gold Record Certificate + Band Yearbook
10. Friday MP3 Recording Emailed to Parents
