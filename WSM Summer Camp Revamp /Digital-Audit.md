# WSM Summer Camp — Full Digital Audit

## The One-Sentence Summary

The program is genuinely good. The website is actively losing you enrollments. The product and the pipeline are disconnected at almost every step.

---

## 🚨 Critical Issues (Losing Enrollments Right Now)

### 1. Your Registration Flow Is a Dead End

This is your single biggest problem. Every "Save Your Spot!" button on the camp page leads to `/camp-signup` — which is just a generic contact form with the message "someone from our team will reach out to you to secure your child's spot."

There is no actual registration. No payment. No session selection. No confirmation. A parent who is ready to spend $425 right now has to fill out a form and wait for a phone call. Industry research is clear that 69% of camp bookings happen on mobile and parents expect to register like ordering on Amazon. You're asking them to leave a voicemail at the DMV instead. This alone is likely your #1 driver of lost conversions.

**Fix:** Implement a real registration and payment platform — Sawyer, Jumbula, Activity Messenger, or even a simple Squarespace product page with session selection and Stripe checkout. Parents need to be able to book and pay in under 3 minutes.

---

### 2. Your Early Bird Offer Has No Urgency Engine Behind It

Your page currently says "EARLY SIGN-UP DISCOUNT! $375 IF ENROLLED BEFORE MAY 15TH, 2026." The date and formatting have been cleaned up (previously showed an earlier deadline and an awkward "Th" capitalization), which is good. The discount itself is meaningful — $50 off ($375 vs. $425).

But the offer is still just a static line of text. There is no countdown timer, no "X days left" banner, no urgency push anywhere on the page. A parent who sees "before May 15th" in mid-April has no emotional reason to act now versus next month — and "next month" is where enrollments go to die. The discount is doing almost none of the work it could be doing.

**Fix:** Add a visible countdown timer or deadline banner above the fold. Layer on email + social reminders as the deadline approaches (T-30, T-14, T-7, T-48hr, final call). Future years: open registration in January, run early bird through February, and use the urgency window more aggressively.

---

### 3. Nine-Day Gap in Your Schedule With No Camp

Sessions run June 15–26, then nothing from June 27 through July 5 (9 days), then resume July 6. That's a blackout during peak Miami summer camp season. Families who want consecutive weeks of coverage will look elsewhere. You're also leaving revenue on the table.

**Fix:** Add a "Session B.5" or a bridge week. Even a lighter programming week at a lower price would fill the gap and keep families from defecting to a competitor.

---

## ⚠️ Major Issues (Hurting Conversion & Trust)

### 4. Your Gallery Is One Session Linked to Google Drive

The Camp Gallery page has a single thumbnail for "Summer Camp Session A 2025" that links to a Google Drive folder. That's it. No video. No Session B–G photos. No parent quotes. No performance clips. Sending parents to your Google Drive is the digital equivalent of showing someone a filing cabinet as a portfolio.

A summer camp gallery is your most powerful sales tool — it lets parents see their kid's future experience. Right now it's doing the opposite: it signals that you ran one session and didn't bother documenting the rest.

**Fix:** Embed a real photo grid directly on the page for each session. Record a 60–90 second highlight video from one Friday showcase and put it at the top of the camp page above everything else. Video is the highest-converting content type for camps.

---

### 5. No Social Proof Specific to Camp

Your general testimonials are excellent ("an A team of professional musicians," "coolest recitals," "best music school you will find"). But the camp page has zero testimonials from camp families. Not one parent quote about the camp experience. Not one camper reaction. This is a missed trust signal at the exact moment a parent is deciding whether to spend $425.

**Fix:** After each session, get 3–5 parent quotes specifically about camp. Put them directly on the camp page. Even one great quote above the "Save Your Spot" CTA would move the needle.

---

### 6. No Instructor Profiles on the Camp Page

Parents are trusting you with their child 9 AM to 3:30 PM for a week. They want to know who the instructors are. The camp page mentions "nationally acclaimed musicians" only in passing in the school's general description. There's no face, no name, no bio on the camp page itself.

**Fix:** Add 2–4 instructor spotlights with photos and a single sentence about their credentials. This dramatically increases trust and differentiation.

---

### 7. No Outcomes / "What Will My Child Learn?" Section

The copy describes structure (band rehearsals, sectionals, workshops) but never articulates results. What does a first-time beginner walk away knowing? What does an intermediate player accomplish in a week? Parents are buying outcomes, not schedules.

**Fix:** Add a short section: "After one week at WSM Camp, your child will..." with 4–5 concrete outcomes for both beginners and intermediate players. This is especially powerful for first-time camp families.

---

### 8. No Multi-Session Pricing or Bundle Incentive

There's one price: $425/week. No sibling discount. No multi-session bundle (e.g., "Book 3+ sessions, save $75"). Families who want their kids in multiple weeks get no reward for that loyalty, and there's no financial incentive to commit to more than one session at a time.

**Fix:** Create a multi-session bundle. Even something like "Book 2+ sessions: $400/week" drives higher per-family revenue and commits families earlier in the season — which stabilizes your planning and cash flow.

---

## 📋 Moderate Issues (Polish & Professionalism)

### 9. Duplicate Navigation Entry

In your nav, "Summer Camp" is a dropdown with only one item: "Music Performance Camp." The top-level "Summer Camp" links to `/summercamp`, and "Music Performance Camp" links to `/musicperformancecamp` — but both pages appear to serve the same content. This creates a redundant click and confuses the user journey.

**Fix:** Make "Summer Camp" in the nav go directly to `/musicperformancecamp`. Remove the redundant step.

---

### 10. No Session Availability / Capacity Indicators

There's no way to know if any session is close to full, sold out, or has plenty of spots. Scarcity is one of the most powerful enrollment drivers. Even a simple "4 spots remaining" on popular sessions would create urgency.

**Fix:** Display capacity or availability per session. If you use a real registration platform (see issue #1), this becomes automatic.

---

### 11. No Email Capture for People Not Ready to Commit

A parent who lands on the page in January, likes what they see, but isn't ready to pay yet has no way to stay in the loop. There's no "Notify me when registration opens" or "Join the camp waitlist" option. You're losing warm leads entirely.

**Fix:** Add a simple email opt-in: "Get camp updates & early bird pricing" with a Mailchimp or Squarespace form. These are people who already found your page — they're hot leads.

---

### 12. Aftercare Is Mentioned but Not Merchandised

The aftercare offering ($25/day, 3:30–5:00 PM) appears in two places: as a footnote under the daily schedule and again in the FAQ. So it's not hidden — but it's also not sold. For working parents, aftercare isn't a bonus detail to be disclosed at the bottom of a schedule; it's often the deciding factor in whether camp is even logistically possible. Research shows childcare logistics are a barrier to enrollment for nearly half of parents.

**Fix:** Treat aftercare as a featured parent benefit, not a schedule footnote. Surface it near the price and session list — e.g., "Extended care available until 5 PM — $25/day" as a labeled perk. Same info, dramatically different perceived value.

---

### 13. The "Lunch Break / Recess" Label Undersells the Experience

Your schedule includes "Lunch + Recess" at 12:15. The word "recess" sounds like elementary school busywork. This is a chance to talk about community, bonding, making friends — which is actually one of the top reasons parents choose camps.

**Fix:** Rename it or add a brief description. Even "Lunch + Free Play & Bonding" is better.
